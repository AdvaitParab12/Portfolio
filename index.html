Noughts and Crosses (also known as Tic-Tac-Toe) is a simple two-player game played on a 3x3 grid. One player uses "X" and the other uses "O". Players take turns marking a space on the grid, aiming to place three of their marks in a row—horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. The first player to achieve this wins the game, and if the grid fills up without a winner, the game ends in a draw.
